Help! I'm Spending Too Much Time on Social Media!

March 14, 2026  ·  Jeff Johnson  ·  The Kiln Ministries

You did not plan to spend an hour on TikTok. You never do. But the algorithm is patient, and it seems that it knows you better than you know yourself. If you are a Christian with a smartphone, this article is for you.

Few passages speak more directly to it than Ephesians 5:8-17. Written to believers living in the spiritually dark and sexually permissive city of Ephesus, Paul's words cut to the heart of what it means to walk faithfully in a culture bent on distraction and indulgence.

Three Key Exhortations

Three exhortations emerge from this passage that speak directly to the challenge of social media:

  1. Do not partner with sin (vv. 7-8) — instead, walk in the light (v. 9).
  2. Discern what is pleasing to the Lord (v. 10).
  3. Walk wisely, redeeming your time, "because the days are evil" (v. 16).

Do Not Partner with Sin

Algorithms are engineered to entice — to surface content that is sexually charged, that stokes envy, or that keeps you scrolling for one more hit of outrage or entertainment. When you indulge a creator's sinful content, you are not a passive observer. You are contributing to their platform and livelihood. In a very real sense, you are partnering with them in their sin.

"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?"

Jeremiah 17:9

Be Discerning

Social media has a remarkable ability to dull our spiritual senses over time. Even seemingly harmless content can quietly crowd out the things of God when consumed without discretion. An unchecked diet of partisan news can shift our gaze from the eternal to the anxious, training us to think of ourselves as citizens of a political tribe rather than citizens of the Kingdom.

Redeem the Time

Before you open an app, ask yourself honestly: How will this end? An hour from now, will I be more joyful? More prayerful? More ready to serve my neighbor and love my Savior?

"Look carefully then how you walk, not as unwise but as wise, making the best use of the time, because the days are evil."

Ephesians 5:15-16

God has purposes for your one life. You are called to carry the good news of the gospel to the people around you, to love your family, to build up the church. Every hour surrendered to an algorithm is an hour not given to those things. The days are indeed evil — but they are also short. Use them well.
