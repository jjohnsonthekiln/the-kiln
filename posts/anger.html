Conquering Anger by GRACE

March 14, 2026 · Jeff Johnson · The Kiln

What Is Sinful Anger?

Sinful anger is an emotion rooted in pride, selfishness, or a desire for revenge. It manifests as outbursts, bitterness, and malice — what Scripture often calls "wrath" or "rage."

"What causes quarrels and what causes fights among you? Is it not this, that your passions are at war within you? You desire and do not have, so you murder. You covet and cannot obtain, so you fight and quarrel."

James 4:1–2

How Is This Different from God's Anger?

God's anger is perfectly justified and perfectly controlled. Human anger is frequently sinful, prone to foolishness, and often leads to evil action.

Guidelines for Overcoming Sinful Anger

1. Remember That God Is in Control

"Our God is in the heavens; he does all that he pleases."

Psalm 115:3

"No temptation has overtaken you that is not common to man. God is faithful, and he will not let you be tempted beyond your ability, but with the temptation he will also provide the way of escape."

1 Corinthians 10:13

2. Remember That You Are Not God

"Beloved, never avenge yourselves, but leave it to the wrath of God, for it is written, Vengeance is mine, I will repay, says the Lord."

Romans 12:19

3. Remember Who You Are in Christ

"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."

2 Corinthians 5:17

4. Remember God's Graciousness to You — and Pass It On

"Let all bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor and slander be put away from you, along with all malice. Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you."

Ephesians 4:31–32

The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant powerfully illustrates this point. A servant forgiven an incalculable debt turns and refuses to forgive a fellow servant a trivial one. The grace we have received is the measure by which we are called to extend grace to others.
