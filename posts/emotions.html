Emotions in Biblical Counseling

March 14, 2026 · Jeff Johnson · The Kiln

Emotions are a window into a person's heart, enabling a counselor to gauge spiritual maturity, integrity, and the fruit of the Spirit. Counselors should allow emotions to surface freely, while taking care not to encourage or validate those that are sinful. The fruits of the Spirit listed in Galatians 5:22–23 serve as the primary gauge of righteous emotion.

"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?"

Jeremiah 17:9

Robert Robinson recognized this same reality when he wrote in 1758, "Prone to wander, Lord, I feel it, prone to leave the God I love." What Robinson confessed, genuine believers across the centuries have echoed — our hearts, as the seat of our emotions, require constant renewal from the Lord.

The Spirit's Work in the Heart

Counselees are ultimately helpless apart from the Spirit's work in their lives. When a counselee cannot seem to gain control over lust, anger, or similar besetting emotions, this may indicate they are not yet united to Christ.

"And such were some of you. But you were washed, you were sanctified, you were justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God."

1 Corinthians 6:11

Scripture Over Feelings

We live in a therapeutic culture with deep roots in treating experiential sense perception as the primary guide in life. Believers struggling with sinful emotions must be taught that the objective Word of God holds that place instead. Scripture ought to inform our feelings — not the other way around.

"Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth."

Colossians 3:2

Scripture as the Instrument of Change

Scripture is ultimately the key instrument of heart change. I once counseled a man struggling with anger and a retaliatory spirit in a genuinely difficult work environment. We read Ephesians 4:30–32 together, and the change evident in his life over the following month was extraordinary — a clear testimony to the Spirit's work.

This is why, moment by moment, we return to Christ in faith — empty-handed — trusting that He alone can fill all in all, and that His Spirit is the one building us into the image of His beloved Son.
